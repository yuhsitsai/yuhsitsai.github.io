Cardboard Vending Machine

May '25 | EDES 301

For our final project in EDES 301, our team of three decided to build a simple vending machine. As our summer course was part of the ISEED study abroad program, we had limited access to materials for this project. However, I am pretty pround of how it turned out.

In the project, I was responsible for the electronics and code, connecting a keypad, a lcd screen, and the coin detecting mechanism (a photoresistor and a LED) through an Arduino Mega to the three motors that released the items.

The largest challenge in the project, a least for me, was by far is the wiring. The amount of electrical components we had ended up requiring a huge amount of wires, and with only jumper wires and the need to fit everything into a small box, it took some delicate rewiring to finally fit everything in.
